Cooking Oil Quality Evaluation via Dielectric Constant Measurement Using Capacitive Sensing

Overview
Cooking oil quality is an important factor in food safety and human health. This project focuses on evaluating the quality of cooking oil by measuring its dielectric constant using capacitive sensing techniques. Since the dielectric properties of oil change with repeated usage and temperature variations, dielectric constant can be used as an indicator of oil quality.
The project investigates various measurement techniques and compares their effectiveness in determining the dielectric properties of cooking oil samples.

Objectives
- Evaluate cooking oil quality using dielectric constant measurement.
- Study the relationship between dielectric properties and oil quality.
- Compare different measurement techniques for capacitance and dielectric constant evaluation.
- Analyze the effect of temperature on dielectric constant.
- Develop a low-cost and effective method for oil quality assessment.
